Health effects and sources of air pollution

Can air pollution cause health problems?

How does air pollution affect children?

Air pollution has serious, long-term health consequences for children, impacting their respiratory systems, cognitive development, and overall health. Children are particularly vulnerable because they breathe faster than adults and have developing lungs and brains.

What are the main risk factors of death worldwide?

The leading risk factors for death worldwide include cardiovascular diseases like ischemic heart disease and stroke, respiratory illnesses such as chronic obstructive pulmonary disease and lower respiratory infections, and non-communicable diseases like diabetes and cancer.

Primary Sources of PM2.5 Pollution

Primary sources of PM2.5 pollution include both human-made (anthropogenic) and natural sources. Key anthropogenic sources are combustion processes (like vehicle emissions, industrial activities, and residential heating), construction and demolition, and industrial processes. Natural sources include dust storms, wildfires, and volcanic ash.

Health effects and sources of air pollution

Air pollution significantly impacts human health, causing respiratory illnesses, cardiovascular diseases, and even cancer, while also contributing to global issues like climate change. The primary sources of air pollution include the burning of fossil fuels, industrial emissions, vehicular exhaust, and agricultural practices.
